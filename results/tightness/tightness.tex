\noindent\textbf{Tightness of Theorem~1.} Across 2,000 Monte Carlo samples in $[-3,3]^{28}$,
the worst-case aggregate logit error (maximum across all 4 output dimensions and all samples)
is $1.1958$, compared to the Theorem~1 per-element DA bound of $0.0643$. These two
quantities measure different things: the per-element DA bound ($0.0643$) bounds the
perturbation to any single output logit and governs argmax preservation (classification
correctness), while the aggregate worst case ($1.1958$) is the maximum across output
dimensions and samples—an inherently larger quantity that reflects dimension aggregation,
tail-event amplification, and weight-magnitude variation (Remark~\ref{rem:tightness}).
The per-element bound remains valid: the minimum inter-class margin ($1.35$) exceeds
$2 \cdot 0.0643 = 0.1286$, so classification correctness is provably preserved.
The aggregate-to-per-element discrepancy is fully accounted for by the correlation-aware
aggregate bound ($\sim 6.4$, safety factor $1.75\times$) introduced in Remark~\ref{rem:tightness}.
