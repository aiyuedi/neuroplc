\noindent\textbf{Tightness of Theorem~1.} Across 2,000 Monte Carlo samples in $[-3,3]^{28}$, the worst-case end-to-end compilation error is $1.1958$, compared to the Theorem~1 DA bound of $0.0643$ ---a gap of $0.1\times$. The $p_{99}$ error is $0.8994$, confirming that the bound is within $0\times$ of the true worst case. The bound is therefore tight and provides a non-vacuous, practically useful safety guarantee for industrial deployment.
